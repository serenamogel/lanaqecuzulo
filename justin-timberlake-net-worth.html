Justin Timberlake’s Early Life and Career

Justin Randall Timberlake was born in Memphis, Tennessee on January 31, 1981. He grew raised in Shelby Forest, a little town. From his father’s second marriage, he has two half-brothers and a half-sister who died soon after delivery in 1997.

Timberlake, who was introduced to music at an early age by his grandpa, sang country and gospel songs as a youngster. At the age of 11, he performed on the television program “Star Search,” where he sang under the name “Justin Randall.” He was a regular cast member on “The All-New Mickey Mouse Club” in 1993 and 1994, with Britney Spears, Christina Aguilera, JC Chasez, and Ryan Gosling. Timberlake persuaded Chasez to join him in a male singing ensemble created by manager Lou Pearlman in 1995, which ultimately became the boyband known as NSYNC.
