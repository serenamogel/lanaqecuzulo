Patrick Swayze Was Never Better Than In This Action Movie Classic On Netflix

August 2024 · 5 minute read

Patrick Swayze stars in Road House which is streaming on Netflix

By Doug Norrie | Published 7 months ago

Prepare to buckle up for a thrilling and action-packed ride as the late, great Patrick Swayze takes the lead in the iconic movie Road House now roaring back to life on Netflix. Directed by Rowdy Herrington, this classic 80s action film promises to deliver a high-octane experience, so hop on for a wild adventure through the world of bouncers and barroom brawls.

In Road House, Patrick Swayze delivers an electrifying performance as Dalton, a renowned cooler, hired to tame the rowdiest bars across the country. With his enigmatic charm and martial arts expertise, Dalton becomes a force to be reckoned with, transforming chaos into order wherever he goes. As he takes on the unruly Double Deuce bar, he finds himself entangled in a web of corruption and power struggles.

Patrick Swayze plays Dalton, a renowned cooler hired to tame the rowdiest bars across the country

Amidst the neon-lit nights and raw, gritty fights, Swayze’s portrayal of Dalton shines brightly. His charisma and physical prowess bring authenticity to the character, making Dalton an enduring action hero loved by fans for decades.

Road House is not just about heart-pounding action; it also boasts a strong supporting cast, including Sam Elliott as Wade Garrett, Dalton’s mentor and fellow bouncer, and Ben Gazzara as Brad Wesley, the ruthless and manipulative businessman responsible for the town’s turmoil. The chemistry between Swayze and Elliott adds depth to their brotherly bond, while Gazzara’s villainous performance keeps viewers on the edge of their seats.

Upon its initial release, Road House garnered mixed reviews from critics, with some praising its high-energy action and Swayze’s magnetic presence, while others questioned its plot and character development. However, as years passed, the movie earned a dedicated cult following, becoming a quintessential part of 80s pop culture.

Road House has earned a dedicated cult following over the years.

Although Road House might not have received universal acclaim, it found its niche among action aficionados and continues to be celebrated as a beloved classic. On Rotten Tomatoes, the film currently holds a so-so 39 percent approval rating, reflecting its status as a cult favorite rather than a critical darling.

At the box office, Road House achieved moderate commercial success, raking in approximately $30 million against its $17 million budget. While not a massive blockbuster, the movie’s impact extended well beyond the box office, solidifying Patrick Swayze’s status as a leading action star.

And we will get a return to this world in the form of a Road House reboot. Jake Gyllenhaal will star as an ex-MMA fighter turned bar bouncer. Daniela Melchior and Billy Magnussen will also star in the Doug Liman-directed film.

Patrick Swayze Was A True Hollywood Star

Speaking of Patrick Swayze’s career, Road House stands as one of the actor’s defining roles, showcasing his versatility beyond romantic dramas. From his unforgettable performance in Dirty Dancing to his action-packed role in Point Break, Swayze’s talent knew no bounds, leaving an indelible mark on Hollywood.

Born on August 18, 1952, in Houston, Texas, Patrick Wayne Swayze’s passion for the arts emerged at an early age. His mother, Patsy Swayze, was a renowned dance instructor, and she played a significant role in shaping his love for dance and performance. Swayze’s journey into the world of entertainment began with ballet and eventually transitioned to acting.

His breakthrough role came in 1983 with the romantic drama The Outsiders, directed by Francis Ford Coppola. However, it was his role as dance instructor Johnny Castle in Dirty Dancing (1987) that catapulted him to superstardom. The film became a cultural phenomenon, and Swayze’s mesmerizing dance sequences with co-star Jennifer Grey left an indelible mark on audiences worldwide.

Swayze’s career reached new heights with the action-packed Road House in 1989. The movie showcased his physical prowess and allowed him to demonstrate his ability to excel in intense, adrenaline-pumping roles. Road House cemented his status as a bankable action star, and audiences couldn’t get enough of his charismatic on-screen presence.

Following Road House, Swayze continued to diversify his portfolio, starring in a range of genres, from romantic dramas like Ghost (1990) to action-packed thrillers like Point Break (1991). In Ghost, Swayze’s portrayal of a spirit trying to protect his love, played by Demi Moore, earned critical acclaim and an Academy Award nomination, further highlighting his versatility as an actor.

Despite his successes, Swayze remained grounded and known for his down-to-earth demeanor. He was a devoted family man and maintained a strong work ethic throughout his career. Behind the scenes, he was known for his professionalism, bringing dedication and passion to every project he took on.

As the years passed, Swayze’s health became a topic of concern for his fans. In early 2008, he was diagnosed with pancreatic cancer, a devastating blow to both him and his admirers. Despite the challenging circumstances, Swayze continued to work on projects and display resilience in the face of adversity.

On September 14, 2009, the world mourned the loss of Patrick Swayze, who passed away at the age of 57. His death left a void in Hollywood and in the hearts of millions of fans who had been touched by his performances.

As Road House revs its engines and returns to the spotlight on Netflix, we have the chance to witness Patrick Swayze at his finest – a charismatic, fearless, and unforgettable action star. So, buckle up, and get ready for a wild ride through the world of Road House with Patrick Swayze leading the way.
