Chef Supreme by Martin Ginsburg

Looking for a unique stocking stuffer? How about the new cookbook authored by the spouses of the Supreme Court.

Advertisement

Chef Supreme: Martin Ginsburg is a tribute to Justice Ruth Bader Ginsburg's late husband, Martin Ginsburg.  

The wife of Justice Samuel Alito, Martha-Ann Alito, came up with the idea for the book after Martin Ginsburg's death in 2010, according to Nina Totenberg at NPR. 

Martin Ginsburg was a tax law professor at Georgetown University, but he was also famous for being a wiz in the kitchen.

The amateur chef once told The New York Times, "my wife does not give me any advice about cooking, and I do not give her advice about the law. This seems to work quite well on both sides."

Advertisement

The gourmand whipped up meals for monthly court lunches organized by the Supreme Court spouses.

Among the cookbook's 126 recipes, you'll find instructions for Ginsburg's orange-scented biscotti, wild boar, and French baguettes. 

"'One of my first conversations with Marty, in the fall of 2006, was about food and nourishment, and how satisfying an expression of love that it was for him," she recalls. "And that, in part, led to the idea that we should put the cookbook together,'"Alito tells NPR.

You can purchase the cookbook online for $24.95 through the Supreme Court Gift Shop.
