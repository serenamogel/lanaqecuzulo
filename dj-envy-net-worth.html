Dj Envy | Music Ventures, Shows, and Books

Musical Ventures

DJ Envy started collaborating with big stars like Jay-Z and 50 Cent by the early 2000s.

He signed with Desert Storm and later released his commercial debut album, “The Desert Storm Mixtape: Blok Party, Vo. 1.”

After a short while, Envy started to dabble with the radio world. He began mixing on Hot 97’s “Takin’ it to the Streets” show.

The Breakfast Club

In 2010, DJ Envy, Angela Yee, and Charlamagne Tha God hosted “The Breakfast Club.”

When anchor Angie Martinez took maternity leave, Casey stepped in and proved his abilities as a radio host. He subsequently began his own radio shows.

The show’s popularity among urban residents garnered him considerable fame. Soon after, he landed parts in movies and TV series and even worked as an MTV VJ.

Today, he continues to be active in the music industry while concentrating his efforts on his radio hosting responsibilities.

Books

DJ Envy has also established himself as an author. He released a book he co-wrote with his wife, Gia Casey, on April 19, 2022.

The book is titled “Real Life, Real Love: Life Lessons on Joy, Pain, & the Magic That Holds Us Together.”

Dj Envy | Career

DJ Envy has always had a special fondness for music. He began making a lot of effort toward his passion during his teenage years.

He earned his bachelor’s in business administration from Hampton University in Virginia.

Envy was employed by DJ Clue as his gardener in 1991. He used to do some beatboxing while working as a gardener.

Dj Clue was impressed with the talent of Dj Envy and introduced him to the New York mixtape scene, and the rest is history.
