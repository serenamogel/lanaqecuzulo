The Bogdanoffs are famous identical twins from France. By profession, they are television presenters, essayists, and producers. They have been presenting on subjects in science fiction, popular science, and cosmology since the 70s on TV.

The brothers came to the limelight with the TV series, Temps X, released in 1979. The duo hosted programs on science and science fiction. They introduced English and American sci-fi series to the French public including The Prisoner, Star Trek, and Doctor Who.

The Bogdanoff brothers gained a lot of public attention following the facial surgery in the late 1990s. The medical procedure deformed their face to the extent of being unrecognizable which attracted a lot of criticism from the public.

Why Did The Bogdanoff Twins Get Plastic Surgery?

The Bogdanoff twins opted for plastic surgery as an experiment. The brothers insisted the changes resulted from working with unspecified technologies.

However, the Bogdanoffs themselves never publicly admitted the fact that they underwent plastic surgery. They claimed it to be experimentation.

The Bogdanoffs lived a sophisticated lifestyle. Other than questionable research on quantum physics, the brothers earned a reputation for extreme plastic surgery.

The public was captivated by the length Bogdanoffs went to with the surgery. They extensively used Botox, lip filler, and chin sculpting not once but twice, in the 1900s and in the 2000s.

These surgeries resulted in a huge change in their appearance so and so that they were rendered almost unrecognizable. Some people even labeled them as aliens.

In 2010, the brothers said in a public statement that they were proud to have faces like extra-terrestrials.

They had prominent cheekbones and chins. The Sydney Morning Herald described their chins as high and bulbous that appear to threaten their owner’s vision. The article further added that their appearance at the Cannes Film Festival caused a stir around the world.

Where Are Igor And Grichka Bogdanoff Now?

Igor and Grichka Bogdanoff now rest in peace at Saint-Lary cemetery in southwestern France. Saint-Lary is also the birthplace of the twins.

Grichka took his last breath at a hospital in Paris on December 28, 2021. Similarly, Igor left the world on January 3, 2022.

The New York Times article from January 7, 2022, confirmed the news of their demise.

The Bogdanoffs were 72 years at the time of their passing. The family of the twins confirmed the sad news but didn’t reveal the cause of death.

The brothers were hospitalized at the Georges Pompidou European Hospital on December 15, 2021, in critical condition. It was confirmed that they had contracted COVID-19. Unfortunately, both of them were unvaccinated.

The funeral for the twins was held at the Church of the Madeleine in Paris on January 10, 2022.
